    my take on South Indian Sambar

    • 2 cups red lentils
    • 1 large broccoli, cut in florets
    • 1 cauliflower, cut in florets
    • 2 medium sized yellow or red onions, chopped
    • 3-4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
    • 1 tbsp grated ginger
    • 2 cups of fresh or frozen okras, sliced in two
    • 1 can crushed tomatoes
    • a handful of fresh coriander
    • 1 cup of dry soya chunks, rehydrated
    • 1 Tbsp turmeric
    • 1 Tbsp of ground cumin
    • 3 Tbsp Sambar Masala
        • *can be found in specialized indian supermarket or self made
    • 1 Tbsp ground coriander(1 c. à soupe)
    • 1 Tbsp black mustard seeds (1 c. à soupe)
    • Graines de cumin (1 c. à soupe)
    • 2 cardamom pods (crushed with the side of a knife)
    • 1 fresh chili, chopped finely, or chili flakes
    • 1 tbsp tamarin paste (optional)
    • 2 curry leaves (optional)
    • 1/4 cup neutral vegetable oil or ghee
      Steps
    1. In a large pot, warm your oil or ghee at medium heat. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, cardamom pods and curry leaves. Let the spices fry for a few minutes until they are golden and release a good smell.
    2. Add onions to the pot and cook them, stirring occasionally, for 3-4 minutes.
    3. Add the garlic, ginger and chili to the pot. Let the mixture fry for another 2-3 minutes.
    4. Add the vegetables: brocoli, cauliflower and the chopped stems of the fresh coriander. Continue to cook for 2-3 minutes.
    5. Add lentils and tomatoes. Then, add water to cover to make sure that lentils are well submurged.
    6. Add ground spices: turmeric, cumin, Sambar masala mix and coriander, as well as a good amount of salt and some black pepper. If you have tamarin paste, also add it at this point.
    7. Bring to a boil and then lower the heat to the minimum. Making sure it is simmering.
    8. When the letils are almost fully cooked, add the okras and the soya chunks.
    9. Taste and adjust the seasoning. Depending on the freshness and quality of your spices you might need to add more (old spices are less potent).
    10. Once the lentils are well cooked, you can close heating.
    11. Serve with chopped coriander leaves, and your side of choice (e.g., rice, naan, dosa, idli, chapati or paratha).
